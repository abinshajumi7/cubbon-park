Cubbon Park

Let's Know More About Cubbon Park:

Lush Greenery

The park spans over 300 acres and is home to more than 6,000 trees, making it a paradise for nature lovers

Recreational Activities

Visitors can enjoy walking, jogging, picnicking, and even birdwatching in the serene environment

Statues & Monuments

Several statues of historical figures, including Queen Victoria and King Edward VII, are placed throughout the park

Established in 1870 and named after Sir Mark Cubbon, the park spans over 300 acres and is home to more than 6,000 trees, creating a serene environment for visitors. The park's well-maintained pathways, lush gardens, and towering trees make it a favorite spot for morning walkers, joggers, and nature enthusiasts. It also serves as a hub for cultural and recreational activities, hosting events that celebrate Bengaluru’s rich heritage and vibrant community spirit.

Beyond its natural beauty, Cubbon Park is also a historical landmark, housing several important buildings and monuments. The State Central Library, with its striking red facade, stands as a testament to the city's architectural heritage. Other notable structures within the park include the High Court of Karnataka and the Karnataka Chitra Kala Parishath, which add to its cultural significance. The park’s landscape creatively integrates natural rock formations with grassy expanses and flower beds, making it a picturesque destination for photography and relaxation.

Cubbon Park is not just a place for leisure; it plays a crucial role in Bengaluru’s environmental health. The dense greenery helps regulate the city's air quality, providing a much-needed breath of fresh air amid urban development. The park is also home to a variety of flora and fauna, making it an ideal location for botanists and nature lovers to study different plant species. Its peaceful ambiance attracts professionals, students, and families looking for a quiet retreat, reinforcing Bengaluru’s reputation as the "Garden City" of India.

How To Reach Cubbon Park

The Cubbon Park Metro Station on the Purple Line is the most convenient way to reach the park. The station is just 100 meters away from the park entrance, making it a great option for those looking to avoid traffic.

BY BUS

Bengaluru’s BMTC buses connect Cubbon Park to various parts of the city. You can board a bus heading towards M.G. Road, Kasturba Road, Hudson Circle, or Ambedkar Veedhi, as these locations have stops near the park.

BY METRO

The Cubbon Park Metro Station on the Purple Line is the most convenient way to reach the park. The station is just 100 meters away from the park entrance, making it a great option for those looking to avoid traffic.

By Car or Taxi

If you're driving or taking a cab, Cubbon Park is accessible via M.G. Road, Kasturba Road, and Hudson Circle. Parking is available near the park, but it can get crowded during peak hours.

By Bicycle or Walking

For a more eco-friendly approach, you can cycle or walk to Cubbon Park if you're nearby. The park is pedestrian-friendly, and cycling is a great way to explore its vast greenery.
